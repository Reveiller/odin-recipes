Shanghainese Nian Gao

Description

The Shanghai nian gao style is usually packaged in a thick soft rod to be sliced up or packaged pre-sliced and either stir-fried or added to soup. Depending on the cooking method, this style is a soft to a chewy variant. The Shanghai style keeps the nian gao white, and made with non-glutinous rice. The color is its distinct feature.

When served as a dish, the most common is the stir-fry method, hence the name (炒年糕, chǎo nián gāo). There are three general types. The first is a savory dish, common ingredients include scallions, beef, pork, cabbage, Chinese cabbage etc. The second is a sweet version using standard white sugar. The last version is taste-less, and is often consumed for its chewy textures.

This recipe will be for the first type.

Ingredients

  • Nian Gao
  • Nappa Cabbage
  • Pork (or any protein of choice)
  • Ginger
  • Garlic
  • Scallions
  • Lee Kum Kee Seasoning Soy Sauce (or any soy sauce)
  • Oil
  • Water

Steps

  1. Chop 2-3 stalks of scallions into small pieces.
  2. Cut off two small slices of ginger.
  3. Crush or mince 2-3 cloves of garlic.
  4. Slice your desired amount of pork or protein of choice into small, thin strips.
  5. Marinate meat in small bowl with soy sauce, garlic, ginger, and scallions for at least 2 hours.
  6. Portion out how much nian gao you want and set aside in a bowl with water to soak.
  7. Rip off how many leaves of cabbage you want. Anywhere from 3-5 usually is enough for one serving.
  8. Chop the leaves width-wise to desired thickness.
  9. Pour a small amount of oil into a wok or pan.
  10. Turn on heat and set to high.
  11. After a couple minutes, put meat in and cook until outside seared, but not cooked all the way through.
  12. Take meat out and set aside. Put the cabbage in and cook for a few minutes or just until searing marks are visible.
  13. Once cabbage is thoroughly cooked, drain the water from the nian gao and put in.
  14. Add 2-3 tablespoons of soy sauce. Add more to taste if needed.
  15. Add the partially cooked meat.
  16. Stir fry for a couple minutes. Afterwards, pour in roughly 1-2 cups of water and cover the wok or pan.
  17. Keep covered and let everything steam for about 5 minutes.
  18. Lift cover and check texture of the nian gao. If it is too firm, continue steaming for a couple more minutes.
  19. Scoop into a bowl and serve. Enjoy!
